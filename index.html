Why this matters

YOUR WORK
IS PROPERTY.

The moment you create something original it belongs to you. That ownership is a legal right. But rights only protect you if someone is watching.

What is copyright?

Copyright is the automatic legal right you hold over anything original you create. No registration required. The moment you draw, photograph, write, or design something, you own it. Infringement means someone using your work without permission — and that use may violate your rights under applicable law.

What is infringement?

It happens constantly. Designs appear on print-on-demand stores. Photos get scraped for stock sites. Illustrations end up on merchandise sold for profit. Most creators never find out. We surface it — and we prepare the formal submission requests that notify platforms of the issue.

What is a DMCA notice?

The DMCA gives rights holders — or their authorized agents — the power to submit formal takedown requests. Platforms operating under safe harbor are required to process such requests. We prepare and submit these requests on your behalf, following your review and approval. The platform decides to act. We make sure they are formally notified.

The AI problem

AI companies have scraped billions of images, texts, and designs to train their models — without consent or compensation. Your work may already be inside an AI. We audit this exposure. We document it. As copyright law catches up with technology, that evidence becomes foundational.

Important — How we avoid false positives
We verify before we act. You approve before we send.

Our system cross-references every flagged match against your own registered domains, social handles, and licensed uses — whitelisted during onboarding. If the scan finds your own website, portfolio, or social media, it is automatically excluded. Every genuine flag is reviewed by a human-led IP research workflows before any action is recommended. All recommended actions are reviewed before submission.
